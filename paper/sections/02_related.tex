\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Probing validity and causal probing.}
A linear probe measures correlation, not use: a probe can fit a random control task
\citep{hewitt-liang-2019-designing}, and decodability alone licenses no functional claim
\citep{belinkov-2022-probing}. The information-theoretic view sharpens this: probing recovers
information already present in the inputs \citep{pimentel-etal-2020-information}, and description
length, not accuracy, measures how \emph{extractable} it is \citep{voita-titov-2020-information}.
This is exactly the objection our selectivity test answers: \bc{} is nearly linear in $(B,C)$
on-distribution, so a probe recovers it whether or not the product is represented; the
linear-$(B,C)$ baseline quantifies that inherited decodability and the gap over it is what remains
(\S\ref{sec:dormant}, Prop.~\ref{prop:headroom}). Causal probing goes further by intervening:
amnesic probing removes a property and reads the behavioral effect
\citep{elazar-etal-2021-amnesic}, and concept-erasure methods formalize removing a linear subspace
\citep{ravfogel-etal-2020-null, belrose-etal-2023-leace}; our orthogonal-complement (perp)
intervention is in that lineage, but applied as a single ridge direction inside a full-residual
donor swap at one site, scored by a full-product metric over a dose grid. The failure mode we
document is the interpretability illusion of \citet{bolukbasi-etal-2021-illusion} and, for subspace
activation patching specifically, \citet{makelov-etal-2024-subspace}: a direction can carry a
decodable signal yet be causally disconnected from the output. We adopt activation-patching best
practice throughout \citep{zhang-nanda-2024-towards, heimersheim-nanda-2024-use} and read our
interchange intervention through causal abstraction \citep{geiger-etal-2021-causal}
(\S\ref{sec:dormant}).

\paragraph{Arithmetic and number representations.}
How language models compute arithmetic is increasingly well mapped. Numbers are encoded as
structured directions \citep{wallace-etal-2019-nlp}; addition is implemented with Fourier features
\citep{zhou-etal-2024-fourier} and trigonometric identities \citep{kantamneni-tegmark-2025-trigonometry},
and small models grok modular addition into a clean circuit \citep{nanda-etal-2023-progress,
quirke-barez-2024-understanding}. In large models \citet{stolfo-etal-2023-mechanistic} trace
arithmetic from operand tokens to the final position through late modules, and
\citet{nikankin-etal-2025-arithmetic} argue the computation is a diffuse ``bag of heuristics''
rather than an algorithm. Our result adds a representational corollary to the bag-of-heuristics
picture: the decodable near-answer at the ``$=$'' position is an operand echo, not a stored
intermediate; the answer is assembled from the operand positions at readout, so a value that
decodes at ``$=$'' need not be computed there.

\paragraph{Elicitation and format sensitivity.}
Model accuracy is sensitive to surface form that leaves the answer invariant
\citep{sclar-etal-2024-quantifying}, and mathematical errors in particular track surface structure
and recover under rephrasing \citep{williamson-etal-2025-syntactic, reusch-lehner-2022-extracting}.
That our zero-shot collapse recovers with a few in-context examples is consistent with
demonstrations acting largely through format and priors rather than content
\citep{min-etal-2022-rethinking}; we treat the recovery as a control on the behavioral phenomenon,
not as our contribution, and locate the mechanism question in the zero-shot regime. Closest to us,
\citet{matsumoto-etal-2022-tracing} find decoded intermediate values in neural math solvers to be
causally usable; we find a decodable answer-site subspace that is causally inert, so whether a
decodable value is used is a per-site empirical question.
