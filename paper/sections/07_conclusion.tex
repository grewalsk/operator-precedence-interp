\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

A parenthesized additive identity selectively breaks multiplicative composition in Llama-3.1-8B,
and the tempting reading that the product is represented at the answer site but unused is an
interpretability illusion: the decodable answer-site subspace is operand-dominated and causally
inert, while the answer is re-derived diffusely from the operands. The portable lesson is that a
high decodability $R^2$ requires a baseline and a causal control before it can support any claim
about what a model computes; we release code, data, and the controls so the null and its
positive control can be checked.
