## Headline (the honest, defensible claim)

> In Llama-3.1-8B (base **and** instruction-tuned), in the **failing zero-shot regime** the operand **B
> is highly linearly decodable from the post-bracket `)` residual at *every* layer — including the late
> composition zone** (Instruct CV-R² ≈ **0.98 at the final layer 31**; base ≈ 0.91 mid-late) — **yet the
> model does not compose** `(0+B)*C` (Instruct acc **0.265**, base 0.507). The operand is available at
> its source position throughout the network and is not acted upon (depth-robust, so this is **not** a
> "recent token still in the residual" triviality). Few-shot **repairs composition to ceiling** (0.92)
> with 2–4 in-context examples. The failure is **operation-specific** (addition composes), **depth-**
> and **surface-sensitive**, **magnitude-controlled**, and **unchanged by instruction tuning**.

**Two claims we explicitly do NOT make (the controls killed them):**
1. *Not* "B is encoded identically with and without few-shot" — but also *not* anything mechanistic.
   The by-layer curve (§6) shows few-shot **lowers** mid-late decodability, BUT a length-matched
   non-repairing control proves that drop is **context dilution**, not consumption (`ctx_share ≈ 0.94`
   on base) — so the few-shot decodability contrast carries **no mechanistic signal**.
2. *Not* "the operand is causally discarded." The direct causal patch is inconclusive (§5).

**Net:** every *strong mechanistic* claim was eliminated by its control (the no-op-confound salvage →
inconclusive; the few-shot encoding-vs-use contrast → dilution artifact). What stands is a **behavioral
brittleness characterization** plus a **modest decodable-but-unused observation** (decodability +
behavioral dissociation, no causal clincher) — not a mechanistic centerpiece.

## 3. Brittleness characterization (Branch-B controls + 95% CIs)

**Operation-specific** (the key selectivity result): addition composes, multiplication does not.
- A1 `( 0 + B ) + C =` = **0.995**, A2 `0 + ( B + C ) =` = 0.9275, vs C1 (mult) = 0.265.
- Paired bootstrap: **Δ(A1−C1) = 0.730, 95% CI (0.688, 0.773)** [instruct].

**Magnitude-controlled** — C1 ≪ C4 at *every* matched |B·C| bin (instruct), so it is not "products got
bigger":

| \|B·C\| bin | n | acc(C1) | acc(C4) |
|-----------|---|---------|---------|
| [440,813)   | 79  | 0.532 | 0.962 |
| [813,1185)  | 126 | 0.278 | 0.944 |
| [1185,1558) | 104 | 0.163 | 0.885 |
| [1558,1930) | 64  | 0.188 | 0.891 |
| [1930,2303) | 27  | 0.000 | 0.704 |

**Surface contrast, CI-backed:** Δ(C4−C1) = **0.642, CI (0.595, 0.690)** [instruct]; **0.383, CI
(0.333, 0.433)** [base]. **Depth-sensitive:** D1 `( ( 0 + B ) ) * C =` (redundant nesting, same parse
as C1) = **0.0375** — one extra paren layer crashes it far below C1.

## 4. Few-shot robustness control (the reframing result) → FEWSHOT_FIXES_C1

| tag | 0-shot C1 | 2-shot | 4-shot | C4 ceiling |
|-----|-----------|--------|--------|------------|
| base     | 0.507 | 0.838 | 0.882 | 0.890 |
| instruct | **0.265** | 0.885 | **0.915** | 0.9075 |

Demos are per-item, distinct (B,C) pairs excluding the test pair (no answer leakage). **2–4 worked
examples of the same surface lift C1 to the C4 ceiling** → the zero-shot collapse is an
**elicitation/format fragility, not a binding-capability failure.** This is what retires the strong
"cannot bind the operands" claim and motivates WO#3 (does few-shot change the *encoding* or only the
*use*?).

**What C1 emits instead** (wrong-output distribution): overwhelmingly "other" (wrong products), not
systematic operand-grabbing — instruct: correct 0.265, **other 0.685**, equals_B 0.025, equals_C 0.025,
equals_B+C 0.000; base: correct 0.507, **other 0.460**, equals_B 0.015, equals_C 0.018.

## 5. Direct causal patch (salvage) — INCONCLUSIVE (reported honestly)

The redesigned operand-corruption salvage (corrupt B→B′, patch the clean `)` residual into the
corrupted run, with a site-matched C6 positive control) returned **`n_used = 0` on both tags →
INCONCLUSIVE.** Cause: the model's zero-shot C1 first-token output is **near-invariant to B**
(`n_no_contrast`: instruct 182, base 197 of the sampled C1-wrong pairs), so the contrast-based patch had
no first-token contrast to denoise. That B-invariance is itself *corroborating behavioral* evidence that
B is unused in the failing regime, but it is **not** a clean causal-ablation result. **We therefore do
not assert a direct causal "discarded" claim;** the use-vs-encoding conclusion is established by
elimination (§6).

*(Note: this run's earlier C6→C1-transplant design was a no-op by construction — identical `( 0 + B )`
prefix ⇒ identical `)` residual under causal masking — and was replaced; see `docs/wo2_redesign_note.md`.)*

## 6. WO#3 — by-layer decodability (the curve, not the best layer)

Probe: is B linearly decodable (same dual-ridge CV-R², folds=5, ridge=1.0) from the **test
expression's** `)` residual under shots ∈ {0,2,4}? Site = the **LAST** `)` (the first closes a shot's
bracket); the `( 0 + B )` assertion passed for **all 400 pairs at every shot level (`n_skipped = 0`)**.

The *best-layer* number is R²≈0.99 in every regime — but that is a layer-1–3 value and is **misleading**
(B is a recent token; early-residual probes recover it for free). The load-bearing question is whether B
stays decodable into the **mid-late consumption zone** (layers ≥19, where G4 puts composition ~L30). The
full curve answers it, and the answer is **asymmetric, not "identical in both":**

| regime | L1 | L8 | L16 | L20 | L31 | mid-late min |
|--------|----|----|-----|-----|-----|--------------|
| instruct 0-shot | 0.997 | 0.995 | 0.990 | 0.989 | **0.978** | **0.978** |
| instruct 2-shot | 0.994 | 0.836 | 0.683 | 0.655 | 0.716 | 0.655 |
| instruct 4-shot | 0.994 | 0.633 | 0.556 | 0.503 | 0.659 | **0.503** |
| base 0-shot | 0.998 | 0.996 | 0.964 | 0.925 | **0.919** | **0.911** |
| base 2-shot | 0.995 | 0.828 | 0.753 | 0.764 | 0.755 | 0.755 |
| base 4-shot | 0.995 | 0.637 | 0.575 | 0.488 | 0.717 | **0.488** |

**(a) Zero-shot (FAILS): B is decodable at ~0.91–0.99 at *every* layer, including the final layer 31**
(Instruct 0.978). The operand is present at its site through the whole network — even where composition
should happen — and is still not used. This is the depth-robust decodable-but-not-acted-upon point, and
it survives the recent-token objection (it holds at L31, not just early).

**(b) Few-shot (SUCCEEDS): operand decodability COLLAPSES through the mid layers** (trough ≈ 0.49–0.50 at
L16–20), monotone in shot count, with partial late recovery. So the encoding is **not** preserved across
regimes — the "identical encoding" hypothesis is **falsified.**

**The few-shot drop is DILUTION, not consumption (control settled it).** A length-matched
**non-repairing** few-shot control (same `( 0 + b ) * c =` surface, random same-#digits WRONG demo
answers; cell 82f) reproduces the mid-late drop *without* teaching composition:

| tag | ctrl_acc | d0 (0-shot) | dr (real 4-shot) | dc (control 4-shot) | ctx_share | verdict |
|-----|----------|-------------|------------------|---------------------|-----------|---------|
| base | **0.253** | 0.919 | 0.636 | 0.653 | **0.94** | **R2 DILUTION** |
| instruct | 0.630 | 0.987 | 0.591 | 0.582 | 1.02 | control INVALID (demos partly repaired) |

`ctx_share = (d0−dc)/(d0−dr) ≈ 1` ⇒ the non-repairing prefix accounts for **~all** of the drop; the
control curve lies essentially **on top of** the real few-shot curve (both panels). On **base** (a clean
test — wrong demos did not repair) this is decisive: **R1 consumption is rejected; the mid-late
decodability dip is a context-length artifact.** On **instruct** the control is invalid (the model
composed at 0.63 even with wrong demo *answers* — it uses the demos' *format/surface*, not their values),
but its curve overlap corroborates dilution. **Conclusion: the few-shot decodability contrast carries no
mechanistic signal** beyond the zero-shot decodable-but-unused observation in (a) above.

## Interpretation / framing (locked)

Multiplying a bracketed operand is **brittle, operation-specific, depth-sensitive, surface-sensitive,
and magnitude-controlled** across base and instruction-tuned Llama-3.1-8B, **but recoverable with 2–4
in-context examples**. The mechanistic anchor that survives the controls is: **in the failing zero-shot
regime the operand is decodable at its `)` site at every depth (incl. the final layer) and still not
acted upon** — a depth-robust decodable-but-unused observation. The **stronger** "encoded identically
across regimes → few-shot changes use not encoding" framing is **NOT** supported (few-shot lowers
mid-late decodability; §6), and the lowering was **tested and explained as context dilution, not
consumption** (82f control; R1 rejected on base). There is also **no** direct causal-ablation result
(salvage inconclusive; §5). So the contribution is a **behavioral-brittleness characterization** + a
modest decodability observation, on the decodability vs causal-use line (Hewitt & Liang 2019 probing
controls; Sharma–Dawes–Raval 2026; Nikankin 2024 bag-of-heuristics) — **not** a mechanistic centerpiece.
